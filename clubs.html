Discover Our Student Life

High School Clubs & Extracurricular Activities

Join a School Club

Our school offers a wide variety of clubs that encourage students to explore their interests, develop leadership skills, and make lasting friendships. Whether you love science, sports, arts, or public speaking — there’s a club for you!

Why Clubs Matter

Being part of a club helps students discover new talents, build confidence, and work as a team. Our clubs range from academic, creative, and cultural to sports and community service, ensuring everyone finds a place to belong.

Science & Technology Clubs

Includes the Science Club, Mathematics Club, and ICT & Computer Club — perfect for students who love innovation, problem-solving, and discovery.

Leadership & Service

Student Council, Prefects’ Council, Scouts, and Girl Guides give students opportunities to lead, mentor, and serve the community.

Arts, Media & Culture

Join the Drama Club, Journalism & Media Club, or cultural associations to showcase your creativity and storytelling skills.

Faith & Personal Growth

Christian Union, Young Christian Union, and Peer Counselling Club help students grow spiritually, emotionally, and socially.

Science Club (Biology, Chemistry & Physics)

Based in: Nairobi, Kenya

The Science Club is a hub for curiosity, creativity, and discovery. Members engage in hands-on experiments, research projects, and interactive demonstrations that bring science to life.

Whether dissecting specimens in Biology, mixing colorful chemical reactions in Chemistry, or exploring the laws of motion in Physics, students are encouraged to question, investigate, and innovate.

The club regularly participates in science fairs, exhibitions, and inter-school competitions, providing opportunities to showcase talent and collaborate with peers. With guidance from dedicated teachers, students gain practical skills, critical thinking abilities, and a deeper understanding of the world around them.

"Science is not just about learning — it's about exploring the unknown."
Club Hours

Wednesday: 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM
Friday: 3:30 PM - 5:00 PM

Projects & Activities

Annual Science Fair
STEM Competitions
Weekly Experiments

Membership Info

Open to Form 1–4 students
No fees required
Sign up at the Science Lab

Mathematics Club

Based in: Nairobi, Kenya

The Mathematics Club is a place where numbers come alive and problem-solving becomes an adventure.

Members sharpen their analytical skills through exciting math puzzles, strategic games like chess, and practical applications such as budgeting challenges and coding with algorithms.

The club actively participates in math contests, Olympiads, and inter-school quizzes, fostering teamwork, persistence, and creative thinking. Guided by passionate teachers, students learn to view mathematics not as a subject to be feared, but as a tool to understand and shape the world.

"Mathematics is the language with which God has written the universe."

ICT & Computer Club

Based in: Nairobi, Kenya

The ICT & Computer Club equips students with the digital skills of tomorrow, today.

Members explore **coding, web and app design, robotics, digital art, and cybersecurity**, all while working on real-world projects such as building websites for school events or programming robots for competitions.

The club fosters collaboration through hackathons, problem-solving challenges, and technology exhibitions. With guidance from experienced mentors, students gain both technical skills and the creativity to use them effectively in the modern digital age.

"The best way to predict the future is to invent it."

Debate Club

Based in: Nairobi, Kenya

The Debate Club sharpens students’ minds by nurturing critical thinking, quick reasoning, and persuasive public speaking.

Members engage in debates covering **current affairs, ethics, history, politics, and global challenges**, learning how to research effectively, construct strong arguments, and respond under pressure.

The club also organizes inter-school debate competitions, public speaking workshops, and mock parliamentary sessions — helping students build leadership skills and confidence that last a lifetime.

Student Council / Prefects’ Council

Based in: Nairobi, Kenya

The Student Council is the voice of the students, fostering leadership, teamwork, and responsibility. Members help **organize school events, maintain discipline, mediate between staff and students, and represent the student body’s interests** in decision-making.

They also take part in **leadership training workshops, community service programs, and inter-school leadership forums**, shaping them into confident and capable leaders.
